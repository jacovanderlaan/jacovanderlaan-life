Rotterdam.

Not just where I live — a city I actively like. It was flattened, rebuilt without nostalgia, and never developed the habit of apologising for how it looks.

A city that had to be designed rather than inherited. That is a thing I would like anywhere; here I get to live in it.

Why this city

Most Dutch cities are a preserved past. Rotterdam is a made decision — which is, if you think about it, the same thing this whole site is about.

Amsterdam shows you what was kept. Rotterdam shows you what was chosen. Every skyline here is somebody's argument, and you can still see the seams — which is exactly what makes it readable.

Places I keep returning to

Not a tourist list. The handful that made it into the routine.

Cinema · the favourite

LantarenVenster

Arthouse cinema on the Kop van Zuid. The default answer to "shall we do something?" — and Film & Ontbijt on weekend mornings is the best formula the city has: breakfast at 9:30, then a film chosen by someone with taste.

An island to walk around and a small recreational beach on the Kaag lakes. Ten minutes from the second house, a different pace entirely.
