Things worth owning.

I buy slowly and then stop looking. Most of this list has not changed in years, which is the point — the decision was made once and the thing kept doing its job.

Quality over quantity. Functional and clean. No fast fashion.

The through-line

Scandinavian minimalism, applied to almost everything. Not as a look, as a filter: fewer objects, better ones, nothing shouting for attention.

Louis Poulsen scores highest of anything on this page. Danish lighting design that has been right since the 1950s and still is — the argument for buying once, properly.

Clothing

Premium basics. Two brands do most of the work.

Shirts & shorts

Profuomo

Business casual that survives being worn properly. The Japanese Knitted shirt is the favourite — size 43, bought on sale rather than at €129,95.

Jeans & basics

Diesel

Premium casual and the underwear that gets repurchased without thinking about it. A default, not a discovery.

Interiors

Scandinavian design is the core aesthetic preference — the one thing on this site scored at 0.95.

Lighting · the favourite

Louis Poulsen

Danish, iconic, expensive, worth it. Classic Scandinavian design lighting — the reference point everything else is measured against.

Furniture

BoConcept

Scandinavian and modern without being cold. Where the considered pieces come from.

Furniture · design

Kave Home (La Forma)

Spanish, warmer and more sculptural than the Nordic end of the shelf — arc lamps, a round Belton mirror, chairs with a shape rather than just a function. Where the Scandinavian filter allows itself an exception.

Furniture · functional

IKEA

Not a compromise but a different job: functional, affordable, same design language. Half a house runs on it.

Light as atmosphere

Philips Hue & Nanoleaf

Smart lighting for ambiance rather than gadgetry. Twenty-one Nanoleaf panels from the first Aurora generation, still waiting to go up in the bedroom.

Shops

Dille & Kamille · Søstrene Grene

Rotterdam. Plants, pots, baskets, small Danish deco. Where the last ten percent of a room comes from.

Unglamorous but true

HEMA storage boxes

The London 21L transparent box, bought in bulk and only during a discount round. Order at home is infrastructure too.

Personal care

Consumables settle into a fixed set: it works, so it gets reordered.

Hair

Sebastian Microweb Fiber

Modeling cream, unscented, flexible hold. Not cheap per millilitre; used sparingly.

Shower

Malin + Goetz · AHAVA

Peppermint shampoo and a Dead Sea mineral shower gel. The two-minute luxury at the start of a day.

Face

L'Oréal Men Expert

Hydra Energy against the tired look, plus the Boswelox anti-wrinkle cream. Unromantic, effective.

A note on the mundane: this list exists because it is written down. Refills get bought before they run out and the same set travels in a pre-packed toilet bag. That is not fussiness — it is one decision, taken once, that stops recurring.

What I avoid

A taste profile is defined as much by its exclusions.

No

Fast fashion

Cheap, briefly, repeatedly. The opposite of buying once.

No

Cluttered design

If it needs explaining on the shelf, it will need tidying around for years.

No

Cheap materials

The saving is real and short; the replacement is real and repeated.
