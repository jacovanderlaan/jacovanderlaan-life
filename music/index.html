Trance, disco, and no apologies.

The data architect has a dancefloor. Trance and progressive are the core, disco is the good mood, and there is a shelf of music I like precisely because it is indefensible.

Four hours of a good set does something no productivity book has managed.

The core

Trance and progressive. This is the part I would defend in an argument.

The centre

Trance & progressive

Tenishia, Dart Rayne, Sean Tyas, Susana, Richard Durand, Mike Shiver, Myon, Mark Sherry. Long builds, a break, and a release you can see coming for two minutes — structure you can feel, which may explain a few things.

Dance

Avicii · Gui Boratto · Chris Kaeser

The wider dance shelf. Boratto for the evenings that stay indoors; Avicii for the ones that do not.

Disco

Disco

The oldest good mood there is. Nothing ironic about it — it was engineered to make a room move and it still works.

The indefensible shelf

Every honest taste profile has one. Mine is not small.

Foute muziek

Modern Talking

Absurd, over-produced, and it lands every single time. The Dutch have a word for this category — foute muziek, music that is wrong — and use it affectionately, which is the correct attitude.

Nineties

Eurodance

Big hooks, cheap synths, no shame. The direct ancestor of everything on the trance shelf, wearing worse clothes.

Why this is on a site with a design page on it. A taste profile that is only defensible is a curated pose. I would rather be readable: Louis Poulsen and Modern Talking, in the same person, without either one being a joke.

The rest of the shelf

Pop

Mainstream

Shakira, Maroon 5, Sam Smith, Stromae, Olly Murs. The music that is simply on.

French

Chanson

Aznavour through to Stromae. The one part of this page that would work in a quiet room.

Not music

Brian Tracy

Forty-six tracks of self-development audio that ended up in the music library and tells you as much about me as the rest of it. It belongs on Practice really.

Ibiza

Where the music on this page has an address.

The island

More than the clichés

The island earned its reputation and then got flattened into it. What keeps drawing me is the combination the postcards miss: world-class sets and a quiet interior, the sea at six in the morning and again at six in the evening, and an entire culture built around the idea that a night can legitimately be the point of a trip.

A note on how this list survived

This page exists because of a decision made while clearing out an old hard drive: keep the taste, throw away the files. Twenty-four gigabytes of music went — it is all on streaming anyway — but the artists, tracks and genres were extracted first and kept as data. Three hundred and seventy-two tracks, catalogued. The collection was the disposable part; what it said about me was not.
