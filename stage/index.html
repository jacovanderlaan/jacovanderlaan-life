Contemporary dance.

The passion that is hardest to explain to people who have not sat in front of it. No dialogue, no plot to follow, no soundtrack telling you how to feel — just bodies solving a problem in a room, at a level of precision that takes a decade to reach.

It rewards watching closely. Almost nothing else asks that any more.

The companies

Where the season gets built. Newsletters from all of these arrive; several have for years.

The Hague · the standard

Nederlands Dans Theater

The reason contemporary dance is worth defending as an art form and not a niche. NDT 1 and the younger NDT 2, at Amare — a company Dutch audiences are absurdly lucky to have on their doorstep.

Arnhem

Introdans

Repertoire company with a gift for programming: work you would otherwise have to travel to see, presented without ceremony. Over 75,000 visitors in a season, which for contemporary dance is a real number.

Amsterdam

Dutch National Opera & Ballet

Where the opera half of this page mostly happens. Both art forms under one roof, and a company that keeps letting the two contaminate each other.

Festival

Holland Dance · Julidans

The festivals that bring in what the season does not. Where you see the thing you did not know existed.

The venue

Amare, The Hague

Home of NDT and the Residentie Orkest. A building that took a lot of criticism and turned out to be a very good place to watch dance.

The interim years

Zuiderstrandtheater

Where NDT played while Amare was being built — a temporary hall out by the sea in Scheveningen. Bare, a bit improvised, and several of the best evenings happened there.

Amsterdam · the house

Het Muziektheater

Stopera. The other booking history: opera and ballet on the Amstel, going back years.

Abroad

The Royal Danish Theatre

Copenhagen — ballet and drama, and the one foreign house whose programme I follow from a distance. Consistent with a certain Scandinavian preference elsewhere on this site.

Why dance, specifically

Everything else I care about is mediated — a book, a screen, a file, a model. Dance is the one form where the thing itself is in the room and cannot be reproduced, versioned or backed up. It happens once, at that level, and then it is gone. For someone who spends his working life making things repeatable, that is not a drawback. It is the entire attraction.

Ten years of premieres

Reconstructed from 210 events I marked as attended between 2009 and 2019 — a decade with a very consistent shape.

NDT 1

The premieres

Sehnsucht / Schmetterling (2014) · Strike Root (2015) · Somos (2016) · Stage One (2016) · Spiritwalking (2016) · Side A: Split into One (2017) · Side B: Adrift (2017).

NDT 2

The younger company

Programma A (2013) · Symphysis (2015) · Straight Around (2016) · Symbolen (2016) · Schubert (2017). The company where you see a choreographer before anyone has heard of them.

Abroad

NDT in Berlin

Berliner Festspiele in 2015, Berlin again in 2017, and a dance performance in Paris in 2017. At some point following a company stops being an evening out and becomes a reason to travel.

Ballet

The classical side

Cinderella — Dutch National Ballet (2018) · Balletgala Dansersfonds '79 (2016) · Avond van de Balletmuziek at Carré (2016) · Les Ballets Trockadero de Monte Carlo (2014).

Concert hall

Concertgebouw · Requiem

The Concertgebouw in 2013 and a Requiem concert in Rotterdam the same autumn. The quiet end of the same decade.

The other end

Festivals

Twenty of them: A State Of Trance, Awakenings (two days, sold out, twice), Loveland three years running, Milkshake, Free Your Mind, Mystic Garden, Offer Nissim in Belgium. The dancefloor half has its own page.

What the numbers say. Fourteen NDT evenings, thirteen of them premieres, across ten years — plus twenty festivals in the same period. Contemporary dance and trance, in the same decade, by the same person. Both are built on structure you cannot see until it resolves, which is either a coincidence or the entire explanation.
Source, honestly: these come from Facebook events marked attended, kept as data. The event record runs 2009–2019 and stops there because the platform stopped being where I logged my life — not because the going stopped. Anything after that is still in ticket confirmations waiting to be swept.

What I followed, and when

The same archive holds every page I followed with the date I followed it. It reads like a slow confession of taste.

2012

The two houses, early

Nederlands Dans Theater and Dutch National Ballet, both followed in 2012 — years before most of the premieres above. The interest predates the record of it.

The tell

Batsheva · Sharon Eyal

Following the Batsheva Dance Company and, later, S-E-D (Sharon Eyal's company) is not what a casual viewer does. It is the Gaga-language, Ohad-Naharin end of the field — the choice of someone who went looking past the local season.

Breadth

Opera and the rest

Dutch National Opera, Nationale Opera & Ballet, TranscenDanses, Summer Dance Forever, Rotterdam Danst, Boris Eifman's Anna Karenina. Not one company — a whole corner of the art form.
