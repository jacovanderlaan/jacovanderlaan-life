What I've tried on myself.

Life-hacking, productivity systems, and a handful of teachers I keep returning to. Some of it is method, some of it is not — and I have stopped pretending those are opposites.

The systems came later. The question came first: does this actually feel better?

The method half

Twelve books on productivity, eleven on mental models, five on minimalism. The shelf is honest about where the attention went.

The foundation

GTD — David Allen

Getting Things Done and Making It All Work. The idea that stuck hardest: your head is for having thoughts, not holding them. Everything I build later is a variation on that one sentence.

The correction

Four Thousand Weeks — Oliver Burkeman

The necessary counterweight. You will not get to everything, and a system that promises otherwise is selling something. Kept next to GTD on purpose.

Focus

Deep Work — Cal Newport

Attention as the scarce resource rather than time. The reason a calendar can look empty and a week still be full.

Behaviour

The Power of Habit · Mindset

Duhigg and Dweck. Two books about the same discovery from different ends: what you repeat, and what you believe is changeable.

The unglamorous one

The Checklist Manifesto

Gawande. A checklist is not a lack of expertise; it is what expertise does when the stakes are real. Half my working method is downstream of this.

Systems

Work the System · Everything in Its Place

Carpenter and Charnas. Mise en place for a life. The kitchen metaphor turns out to travel further than expected.

The other half

The teachers. Not a belief system — a set of frames I have found useful enough to keep.

The favourite

Abraham Hicks

The one I return to most. The core idea I actually use: we do things, in the end, to feel good. Emotion as a compass rather than noise to be managed. It is why I work with intentions instead of goals — how something feels is a legitimate measure, not a soft one.

Presence

Eckhart Tolle

The argument that most suffering is time-travel: rehearsing a future or relitigating a past. A useful corrective for someone who builds systems for the next twenty years.

Energy

Anthony Robbins

State, physiology, decision. Less philosophy than the others and more engineering — how you change what you do when you already know what to do. Followed the training arm too (Robbins-Madanes), not just the stage show.

The one you do, not read

Landmark

Not a book but a room. Assisting was the part that taught most: the difference between having an insight and being accountable for one in front of other people.

Stillness

Pema Chödrön · Michael Singer

When Things Fall Apart and Living Untethered. What the productivity shelf has nothing to say about: the weeks that do not go well.

On a wall

The Holstee Manifesto

"This is your life. Do what you love, and do it often." A poster, not a philosophy — and it has outlasted a lot of more sophisticated things.

Why both halves are on one page. For a long time I kept the method and the rest apart, as if a data architect was allowed one and not the other. They answer the same question from different sides: what makes a day worth having. Abraham Hicks says do what feels good; GTD says get it out of your head so you can. That is not a contradiction — it is a division of labour.

Four things I actually believe

Not aspirations. The convictions underneath most of the choices on this site.

01

Growth mindset

Ability is not a fixed allocation you discover, it is something that moves. The reason I keep starting things I am not yet good at — at 58, on a sabbatical, building software I was never trained to build.

02

Lifelong learning

Not a slogan on a CV. There is a learning plan, there are certifications in progress, and the shelf keeps growing. Stopping learning and getting old are, as far as I can tell, the same event.

03

Positive thinking

Less naive than it sounds. What you look for determines what you find, and where attention goes matters more than most planning does. Closest to Abraham Hicks of anything here.

04

Communication is everything

The best model in the world does nothing if nobody understands what it says. Almost every failure I have watched up close was a communication failure wearing a technical costume.
