Living principles

Do It Now

Act while the impulse is warm: the small thing done now never becomes the big thing you dread.

Deferral has a hidden cost that the moment never shows you. A task put off doesn't just wait — it accrues. It has to be re-remembered, re-decided, carried as low-grade background weight, and it tends to swell: the two-minute reply becomes an awkward overdue one, the small fix becomes a dreaded chore. Do it now is the habit of closing the loop while it's cheap — if it takes a moment, do the moment; if it's bigger, take the first real step before the impulse cools. It isn't frantic busyness, and it isn't the enemy of First Things First — order still decides what earns "now". It's the refusal to let doable things rot into a backlog of dread. The counterweight is honesty: some things genuinely should wait, and "now" for everything is just reactivity. But for the pile of small, clear, doable things, the best time is almost always the moment you noticed them.
